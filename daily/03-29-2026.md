- List some common architectural models for access control
- TCSEC's Trusted Computing Base
	- Older Department of Defense standard.
	- Part of a larger series called the Rainbow Series.
	- Replaced in 2005 with Common Criteria
	- Uses concept of Trusted Computing Base (TCB)
		- contains an object known as reference monitor
			- piece of system that manages access controls
		- Challenge of TCB was covert channels
			- covert channels were used by attackers to steal sensitive data
				- Two different kinds of covert channels
					- Storage, and Timing.
- The Information Technology System Evaluation Criteria (ITSEC)
	- International set of criteria.
	- Targets of Evaluation (ToE) are compared to security standards.
- The Common Criteria
	- The Common Criteria for Information Technology Security Evaluation
	- international standard
	- combined effort of contributors from Australia, New Zealand, Canada, France, Germany, Japan, the Netherlands, Spain, the United Kingdom, and the United states
- Bell-LaPadula
	- state machine reference model
		- model of automated system that modifies its state over time
	- uses mac addresses, data classification, and security clearances.
- Biba Integrity Model
	- ensures no information from a subject can be passed to an object at a higher security level
		- prevents contaminating data of  higher integrity with data of lower integrity
- Clark-Wilson Integrity Model
	- Principles of change instead of integrity levels.
	- Internal consistency
- Graham-Denning Access Control Model
	- set of objects, subjects, and rights
- Harrison–Ruzzo–Ullman Model
	- allows changes to access rights and the addition and removal of subjects and objects
- Brewer–Nash Model
	- commonly known as a Chinese wall
	- designed to prevent a conflict of interest between two parties
